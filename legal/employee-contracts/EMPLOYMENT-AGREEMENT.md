## 5. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Confidential Information
During and following employment, the Employee shall:
1. Maintain in strict confidence all non-public information relating to the Company, the Platform, its clients, partners, and stakeholders;
2. Not disclose or use Confidential Information except as required for the performance of duties;
3. Return all Company property and Confidential Information upon termination.

### 5.2 Company IP
All intellectual property developed by the Employee in connection with their employment ("Company IP") shall be and hereby is irrevocably assigned to the Company. The Employee shall execute all documents necessary to perfect such assignment.

---

## 6. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

### 6.1 Non-Compete
For a period of twelve (12) months following termination (regardless of cause), the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:
1. Carry on or be engaged in any competing business;
2. Have more than a [1% / 5%] passive equity interest in a competing entity;
3. Solicit or serve any client or customer of the Company with respect to competing products or services.

### 6.2 Non-Solicitation
For a period of twelve (12) months following termination, the Employee shall not:
1. Solicit, hire, or attempt to influence any employee, contractor, or consultant of the Company;
2. Solicit or attempt to disrupt any contract or relationship with any vendor, partner, or service provider of the Company.

### 6.3 Savings
The non-compete and non-solicitation shall apply only in jurisdictions where the Company operates and shall be limited to the extent permitted by applicable law. If any court finds any portion unenforceable, the scope shall be automatically modified to the maximum enforceable extent.

---

## 7. TERMINATION

### 7.1 Termination by the Company
The Company may terminate this Agreement:
1. **For Cause**: Immediately upon written notice, with no severance, if the Employee:
   a. Is convicted of any criminal offence involving fraud, dishonesty, or moral turpitude;
   b. Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within fourteen (14) days of written notice;
   c. Engages in misconduct, gross negligence, or wilful insubordination;
   d. Discloses Confidential Information without authorisation;
   e. Violates any applicable anti-corruption, AML, or anti-discrimination law.
2. **Without Cause**: Upon [four (4) / two (2)] weeks' written notice. The Company may pay salary in lieu of notice.

### 7.2 Termination by the Employee
The Employee may resign by providing [four (4) / two (2)] weeks' written notice. The Company may place the Employee on garden leave during the notice period.

### 7.3 Payments on Termination
Upon lawful termination, the Company shall pay:
1. All accrued and unpaid base salary to the date of termination;
2. All accrued and unused annual leave;
3. Any properly documented expenses incurred prior to termination.

No other payments shall be due unless expressly provided in this Agreement or required by applicable law.

### 7.4 Garden Leave
During any notice period, the Company may place the Employee on garden leave, during which the Employee shall remain employed but shall be relieved of all duties and shall not access Company systems or represent themselves as a representative of the Company.

---

## 8. POST-TERMINATION OBLIGATIONS

### 8.1 Knowledge Transfer
Upon termination, the Employee shall:
1. Deliver to the Company all documents, files, and materials in their possession or control;
2. Provide a handover of all pending work and responsibilities;
3. Return all Company property within seventy-two (72) hours of termination or garden leave;
4. Provide a signed declaration confirming compliance with the return obligations.

### 8.2 Ongoing Obligations
The following shall survive termination:
1. Confidentiality obligations (indefinite, subject to exceptions);
2. Non-compete and non-solicitation (as specified in Section 6);
3. Intellectual property assignment obligations (indefinite);
4. Non-disparagement obligations (indefinite).
