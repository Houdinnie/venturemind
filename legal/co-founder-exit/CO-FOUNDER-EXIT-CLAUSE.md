## RECITALS

**WHEREAS** the Parties have established a technology company within the VentureMind/Nomad Flow ecosystem (the "Company");

**WHEREAS** the Parties acknowledge that the nature of early-stage technology ventures involves a material risk of Co-Founder departure through resignation, removal, incapacity, or disagreement;

**WHEREAS** the Parties wish to ensure that the Company's operations, platform development, and stakeholder interests are protected regardless of which Co-Founder departs, under what circumstances, or at what time;

**WHEREAS** the Parties wish to eliminate any and all ambiguities, loopholes, or procedural gaps that a departing or remaining Co-Founder might exploit to the detriment of the other or the Company;

**WHEREAS** the Parties acknowledge that this Clause is designed to be enforceable across multiple jurisdictions and that its provisions have been drafted with that cross-border enforceability in mind;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and obligations set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

## SECTION 1 — COMPREHENSIVE EXIT TRIGGERS

### 1.1 Voluntary Departure Without Good Reason
A Co-Founder may voluntarily depart the Company by resignation without Good Reason, subject to the following mandatory conditions, all of which must be satisfied:

1. **Written notice**: The departing Co-Founder must deliver written notice of resignation to the remaining Co-Founder and the Company Secretary (if any) no less than sixty (60) days prior to the intended departure date. The notice must be delivered by registered mail with international courier confirmation and email with read receipt.

2. **Garden leave**: During the sixty (60)-day notice period, the Company may, at its sole election, place the departing Co-Founder on garden leave for any or all of such period, during which the departing Co-Founder shall:
   a. Be relieved of all duties and removed from all systems, communications, and access credentials;
   b. Not represent themselves as a director, officer, employee, or agent of the Company;
   c. Remain on the Company payroll at their full base salary;
   d. Remain bound by all confidentiality, non-compete, non-solicitation, and intellectual property obligations under this Clause and the Founder Agreement.

3. **Full knowledge transfer**: During the notice period, the departing Co-Founder shall:
   a. Prepare and deliver to the Board a comprehensive written handover document covering all active projects, pending obligations, client relationships, key contacts, vendor agreements, and any outstanding legal or regulatory matters;
   b. Participate in a minimum of three (3) full-day knowledge transfer sessions with the remaining Co-Founder or a nominee designated by the Board;
   c. Make themselves available for questions by the Compliance Auditor Agent and the Board for a period of up to thirty (30) days following departure;
   d. Provide the Board with all passwords, encryption keys, domain registrars, hosting credentials, code repositories, and any other access credentials to the Company's systems, within forty-eight (48) hours of being placed on garden leave.

4. **Vesting consequences**: Upon a voluntary departure without Good Reason:
   a. All unvested shares shall be immediately and irrevocably forfeited as of the date of departure;
   b. No severance, payment in lieu of notice, or any other compensation shall be due beyond accrued salary and properly documented reimbursable expenses;
   c. The departing Co-Founder shall have no claim to any additional equity, consideration, or rights beyond their vested shares.

### 1.2 Voluntary Departure With Good Reason
A Co-Founder may resign for Good Reason, as defined in the Founder Agreement. The burden of establishing Good Reason rests entirely with the departing Co-Founder, who must demonstrate:

1. That a condition constituting Good Reason (as enumerated in the Founder Agreement) existed;
2. That the departing Co-Founder notified the Company in writing within ninety (90) days of first becoming aware of such condition;
3. That the Company had a reasonable opportunity to cure the condition (within the cure period specified in the Founder Agreement) before the resignation was submitted.

Failure to comply with the foregoing procedural requirements shall result in the resignation being treated as a voluntary departure without Good Reason for all purposes of this Clause.

### 1.3 Removal for Cause
A Co-Founder may be removed for Cause pursuant to the procedure set forth in the Founder Agreement. Removal for Cause shall result in:

1. Immediate cessation of all roles, duties, and powers as a Co-Founder, director, and officer;
2. Immediate revocation of all system access credentials, corporate cards, and Company property;
3. Forfeiture of all unvested shares as of the date of removal;
4. No severance payment, garden leave, or notice period beyond what is mandated by applicable law;
5. Indemnification of the Company and the remaining Co-Founder from any losses, claims, or liabilities arising from the conduct that gave rise to the Cause finding.

### 1.4 Removal Without Cause
A Co-Founder may be removed Without Cause pursuant to a Board resolution passed by the affirmative vote of a majority of directors excluding the affected Co-Founder. Removal Without Cause shall entitle the removed Co-Founder to:

1. A severance payment equal to twelve (12) months of the removed Co-Founder's then-current base salary, payable in equal monthly installments over twelve (12) months, subject to the removed Co-Founder's compliance with post-departure obligations;
2. Accelerated vesting of twenty-five percent (25%) of the removed Co-Founder's unvested shares;
3. Continued medical and dental insurance coverage (or a cash equivalent) for twelve (12) months following removal;
4. Reimbursement of all properly documented business expenses incurred prior to the date of removal;
5. Outplacement services (up to $5,000 USD) for a period of three (3) months following removal.

### 1.5 Incapacity or Death
In the event that a Co-Founder:
1. Dies;
2. Suffers a physical or mental incapacity that renders them incapable of performing their duties for a continuous period exceeding ninety (90) days; or
3. Is declared legally incapacitated by a court of competent jurisdiction;

then:
a. All unvested shares held by the affected Co-Founder shall vest in full;
b. In the case of death, the estate of the deceased Co-Founder shall receive the deceased's vested shares and any severance owed, subject to applicable succession law;
c. The remaining Co-Founder shall have the exclusive right (but not the obligation) to acquire the deceased or incapacitated Co-Founder's vested shares at fair market value as determined by an independent third-party valuator;
d. The Company shall maintain key-person insurance on each Co-Founder in an amount no less than $250,000 USD, with the proceeds payable to the Company.

## SECTION 2 — IP AND ASSET CLawback

### 2.1 Pre-Departure IP Audit
Prior to the effective date of departure, the Board shall cause the Compliance Auditor Agent to conduct a comprehensive audit of all intellectual property developed, contributed, or conceived by the departing Co-Founder during their engagement with the Company (the "IP Audit"). The IP Audit shall:

1. Identify all source code repositories, documentation, designs, and materials created by the departing Co-Founder;
2. Verify that all such materials have been properly assigned to the Company pursuant to the Founder Agreement;
3. Identify any materials that were not properly assigned or that may be subject to third-party claims;
4. Produce a written report (the "IP Audit Report") delivered to the Board and the departing Co-Founder within fourteen (14) days of the commencement of the IP Audit.

### 2.2 Clawback for Unassigned IP
If the IP Audit reveals any intellectual property that was developed by the departing Co-Founder in connection with the Company or the Platform that was not properly assigned to the Company pursuant to the Founder Agreement:
1. The departing Co-Founder shall, within seven (7) days of notification, execute all documents necessary to complete such assignment;
2. If the departing Co-Founder fails to do so, the Company shall be entitled to seek specific performance and injunctive relief without the requirement to post a bond;
3. In addition, the departing Co-Founder shall pay the Company a sum equal to $25,000 USD per item of unassigned intellectual property discovered, as a contractual estimate of damages.

### 2.3 Company Property Return
Upon departure, the departing Co-Founder must return the following within seventy-two (72) hours of being placed on garden leave or upon written request by the Board, whichever is earlier:

1. All Company-issued hardware (laptops, phones, servers, hardware keys);
2. All access credentials (including but not limited to: cloud hosting accounts, code repositories, domain registrars, SaaS subscriptions, API keys, and any other system or service accessed using Company resources);
3. All physical and electronic documents, files, and data storage devices containing Company information;
4. All corporate cards, keys, identification documents, and any other Company property;
5. A signed declaration (in the form attached as Schedule B) confirming that all such property and credentials have been returned.

Failure to return property or comply with the declaration requirement shall result in:
a. The Company being authorised to immediately disable all access credentials without notice;
b. The departing Co-Founder remaining liable for any losses or damages arising from unauthorised access following departure;
c. The Company being entitled to withhold any payments otherwise due to the departing Co-Founder until compliance.

## SECTION 3 — COMPETITION AND RESIDUAL INTEREST PROTECTION

### 3.1 Expanded Non-Compete — Market-Specific
For a period of eighteen (18) months following departure (regardless of cause), the departing Co-Founder shall not, directly or indirectly, whether through any entity or as an individual:

1. In any jurisdiction where the Company has active users, paying customers, or active partnerships (as documented in the Company's most recent quarterly report):
   a. Develop, sell, license, or provision a competing platform, product, or service that serves the same or substantially similar target market as the Platform;
   b. Hold more than a one percent (1%) economic interest (passive or active) in any competing entity;
   c. Act as a consultant, advisor, contractor, or employee to any competing entity;
2. Use or disclose any confidential information, trade secrets, or non-public business intelligence obtained during engagement with the Company;
3. Represent to any third party that they are affiliated with, endorsed by, or acting on behalf of the Company or the Platform;
4. Attempt to register any trademark, domain name, or social media handle that is identical or confusingly similar to any trademark, brand name, or identifier used by the Company or the Platform.

### 3.2 Market Presence Presumption
For the purposes of Section 3.1(1), the Company shall be presumed to have "active presence" in any jurisdiction where its Platform has been downloaded, accessed, or used by more than one hundred (100) unique users or where the Company has filed regulatory, tax, or corporate registration documents. This presumption may be rebutted by the departing Co-Founder only upon production of written evidence to the contrary.

### 3.3 Competing Activity Clawback
If, during the eighteen (18)-month non-compete period, the Company determines that the departing Co-Founder has engaged in competing activity:
1. All unvested shares (if any remain outstanding) shall be immediately forfeited;
2. The departing Co-Founder shall pay the Company a material breach fee of $100,000 USD, agreed as a genuine pre-estimate of loss and not a penalty;
3. The Company shall be entitled to injunctive relief and specific performance without the requirement to post a bond;
4. The departing Co-Founder shall be liable for the Company's full legal costs and disbursements in enforcing this Clause, on a solicitor-client basis.

## SECTION 4 — DEPARTURE EQUITY VALUATION AND BUYOUT

### 4.1 Departure Valuation
Upon any departure (whether by resignation, removal, death, or incapacity), the value of the departing Co-Founder's equity shall be determined as follows:

1. **Fair Market Value (FMV)**: The equity stake shall be valued at its Fair Market Value, defined as the price at which a willing buyer and a willing seller, each having knowledge of all relevant facts and under no compulsion to buy or sell, would agree;
2. **Valuator**: FMV shall be determined by an independent third-party valuator agreed upon by both parties within fourteen (14) days of departure, or, failing agreement, appointed by the President (or equivalent) of the relevant professional body in the jurisdiction of the Company's primary operations;
3. **Cost of Valuation**: The cost of the valuator shall be borne by the Company;
4. **Timeline**: The valuator shall deliver its determination within sixty (60) days of being appointed.

### 4.2 Buyout Mechanics
Following valuation:
1. The remaining Co-Founder(s) and/or the Company shall have the right (but not the obligation) to acquire the departing Co-Founder's vested shares at FMV;
2. If the remaining Co-Founder(s) elect to acquire the shares, they shall pay the purchase price in cash within ninety (90) days of the valuator's determination;
3. If the remaining Co-Founder(s) do not elect to acquire the shares within thirty (30) days, the Company shall have the right to repurchase the shares at FMV within the subsequent thirty (30) days;
4. If neither the remaining Co-Founder(s) nor the Company elect to acquire the shares, the departing Co-Founder (or their estate) shall retain the shares and may dispose of them in accordance with this Clause and the Founder Agreement.

### 4.3 Deferred Payment
If the remaining Co-Founder(s) or the Company elect to acquire the departing Co-Founder's shares but lack sufficient cash to pay the full FMV at closing:
1. The purchase price may be paid in installments over a period not to exceed twenty-four (24) months;
2. The deferred amount shall accrue interest at a rate of eight percent (8%) per annum;
3. The unacquired shares shall be held as security for the deferred payment and shall be subject to a lien in favour of the departing Co-Founder (or their estate);
4. In the event of a subsequent Change of Control before the deferred amount is paid in full, the entire outstanding balance shall become immediately due and payable.

## SECTION 5 — DEPARTURE ANNOUNCEMENT AND COMMUNICATIONS

### 5.1 No Disparagement
Both during engagement and following departure (regardless of cause), neither Co-Founder shall:
1. Make any statement (whether written, oral, or electronic) that is critical, disparaging, or damaging to the other Co-Founder, the Company, or the Platform;
2. Disclose to any third party the terms and conditions of either Co-Founder's departure;
3. Respond to media inquiries about the departure without the prior written consent of the other Co-Founder and the Board.

### 5.2 Coordinated Departure Announcement
Upon any Co-Founder departure, the Parties shall cooperate to issue a mutually agreed-upon press release, social media statement, and investor communication. The announcement shall:
1. Be neutral in tone and factually accurate;
2. Not attribute fault to either party;
3. Not disclose the terms of departure, equity amounts, or any confidential financial information;
4. Be issued within seven (7) days of the effective departure date.

Violation of this Section shall constitute a material breach of this Clause and shall entitle the non-breaching Party to liquidated damages of $10,000 USD per violation.

## SECTION 6 — ONGOING OBLIGATIONS SURVIVAL

### 6.1 Indefinite Obligations
The following obligations shall survive the termination of a Co-Founder's engagement without time limit:
1. Confidentiality obligations (subject to the exceptions in the Founder Agreement);
2. Indemnification obligations;
3. Intellectual property assignment and moral rights waiver obligations;
4. Third-party IP indemnification obligations.

### 6.2 Time-Limited Obligations
The following obligations shall survive for the periods specified below:

| Obligation | Survival Period |
|-----------|----------------|
| Non-compete | 18 months from departure |
| Non-solicitation of employees and contractors | 12 months from departure |
| Non-solicitation of customers and partners | 12 months from departure |
| Non-disparagement | Indefinite |
| Return of Company property | 7 days from departure |
| Cooperation with litigation/regulatory matters | 5 years from departure |

### 6.3 No Termination of Obligations Upon Breach
No breach of this Clause by the Company or the remaining Co-Founder shall entitle the departing Co-Founder to unilaterally terminate any surviving obligation, nor shall any such breach be used as a set-off against the Company's or the remaining Co-Founder's rights under this Clause.

## SECTION 7 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION FOR THIS CLAUSE

### 7.1 Same Framework as Founder Agreement
Any dispute arising under or in connection with this Clause shall be subject to the dispute resolution provisions of the Founder Agreement.

### 7.2 Interim Relief
Notwithstanding the arbitration agreement in the Founder Agreement, either Party may seek urgent injunctive or other interim relief from the courts of the jurisdiction specified in the Founder Agreement, without such action constituting a waiver of the right to arbitration.
