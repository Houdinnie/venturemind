## SECTION 2 — SHARE CAPITAL

### 2.1 Authorised Share Capital
The Company shall have an authorised share capital of _______________ divided into _______________ shares of no par value each.

### 2.2 Rights Attached to Shares
Unless otherwise provided in these Articles:
1. Each ordinary share confers one vote on a poll;
2. Each ordinary share confers entitlement to an equal dividend;
3. Each ordinary share confers entitlement to an equal participation in any surplus upon winding up;
4. Shares may be issued as fractional shares, with fractionally held shares carrying the corresponding fractional rights.

### 2.3 Alteration of Share Capital
The Company may by ordinary resolution:
1. Increase its share capital by issuing new shares;
2. Consolidate or divide its existing shares;
3. Cancel any shares not taken or agreed to be taken by any person;
4. Reduce its share capital in any manner permitted by applicable law.

## SECTION 3 — DIRECTORS

### 3.1 Number
The Company shall have a minimum of one (1) Director and a maximum of _______________ Directors.

### 3.2 Powers of Directors
The Directors shall manage the business and affairs of the Company and may exercise all powers of the Company that are not reserved to the Shareholders by applicable law or these Articles.

### 3.3 Directors' Duties
Each Director owes duties of:
1. Good faith and in the best interests of the Company;
2. Care and diligence;
3. Avoidance of conflicts of interest;
4. Not fettering future discretions;
5. Acting within authority.

### 3.4 Rotation
At each annual general meeting, one-third of the Directors (or the number nearest one-third) shall retire by rotation and may offer themselves for re-election.

### 3.5 Proceedings
A Director may participate in board meetings by telephone or video conference. A resolution in writing signed by all Directors entitled to vote is as valid as a resolution passed at a board meeting.

### 3.6 Quorum
The quorum for board meetings shall be _______________.

## SECTION 4 — SHAREHOLDER MEETINGS

### 4.1 Annual General Meeting (AGM)
The Company shall hold an AGM once in each calendar year, within [__] months of the Company's financial year end. The AGM shall consider the annual accounts, elect directors, appoint auditors, and address any other ordinary business.

### 4.2 Extraordinary General Meetings (EGM)
EGMs may be convened by the Directors or by Shareholders holding not less than [__]% of the voting rights.

### 4.3 Notice
Notice of general meetings shall be given not less than [__] clear days before the meeting. The notice shall specify the place, date, time, and general nature of business to be conducted.

### 4.4 Voting
On a show of hands, each Shareholder present has one vote. On a poll, each Shareholder has one vote per share held.

## SECTION 5 — DIVIDENDS

### 5.1 Declaration
Dividends may be declared by the Shareholders upon recommendation of the Directors. The Directors may also declare and pay interim dividends without Shareholder approval if, in their judgment, the Company's financial position justifies such payment.

### 5.2 Entitlement
Dividends are payable to Shareholders on the register at the date the dividend is declared or at a date fixed by the Directors, whichever is later.

### 5.3 Unclaimed Dividends
Unclaimed dividends returned to the Company after [__] years shall be forfeited and shall become part of the Company's reserves.

---

## SECTION 6 — TRANSFER OF SHARES

### 6.1 Transfer Restrictions
Shares are subject to the transfer restrictions set out in the Shareholders' Agreement. Any transfer in violation of the Shareholders' Agreement is void.

### 6.2 Transfer Procedure
A share transfer must be:
1. Effected by an instrument of transfer in the form prescribed by the Directors;
2. Signed by the transferor and transferee;
3. Accompanied by the relevant share certificate(s);
4. Approved by the Directors (if their approval is required under the Shareholders' Agreement).

### 6.3 Lien
The Company has a first and paramount lien on any share that is partly paid. The lien covers unpaid calls, instalments, and any amounts owed to the Company in connection with the share.

## SECTION 7 — ACCOUNTS AND AUDIT

### 7.1 Books of Account
The Directors shall cause proper books of account to be kept in accordance with applicable law.

### 7.2 Audit
The Company's accounts shall be audited by an independent auditor appointed at each AGM.

### 7.3 Annual Accounts
The Directors shall prepare annual accounts and lay them before the AGM.

---

## SECTION 8 — WINDING UP

### 8.1 Distribution of Surplus
If the Company is wound up, the liquidator shall distribute the Company's assets in the following order:
1. **First**: To the Company's creditors (preferred and secured creditors in priority);
2. **Second**: To preference Shareholders (if any) up to the amount paid up on their shares;
3. **Third**: To the ordinary Shareholders pro-rata to their shareholdings.

### 8.2 Limited Liability
No Shareholder is liable to contribute to the assets of the Company in a winding up beyond the amount, if any, unpaid on their shares.

---

## SECTION 9 — INDEMNITY

### 9.1 Indemnity
The Company shall indemnify every Director, Officer, Employee, and Agent against all costs, charges, losses, expenses, and liabilities incurred in the performance of their duties.

### 9.2 Exceptions
This indemnity does not cover acts of dishonesty, fraud, wilful misconduct, or gross negligence.
