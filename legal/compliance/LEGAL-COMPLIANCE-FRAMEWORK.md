# LEGAL COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK
## VentureMind — Nomad Flow Ecosystem
### Version 1.0 | Effective Date: _____________, 2026

---

## PURPOSE

This Legal Compliance Framework ("Framework") establishes the minimum compliance obligations for the Company in connection with the operation of the VentureMind/Nomad Flow platform. It is designed to withstand scrutiny from regulators, external auditors, and legal counsel in any jurisdiction in which the Company operates or serves Users.

This Framework is not a substitute for jurisdiction-specific legal advice. The Company shall maintain relationships with qualified legal counsel in each jurisdiction in which it operates or in which its Users are resident.

---

## SECTION 1 — ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML)

### 1.1 Obligations
The Company shall comply with all applicable AML laws and regulations, including but not limited to:
- EU Anti-Money Laundering Directive (AMLD6 and subsequent);
- US Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and USA PATRIOT Act;
- UK Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing, and Transfer of Funds Regulations 2017;
- Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations;
- Any applicable AML legislation in jurisdictions where the Company has entities, operations, or Users.

### 1.2 KYC Requirements
All Users who engage the Company for services involving the formation of legal entities, management of client funds, or execution of transactions must complete KYC verification prior to engagement. KYC requirements are detailed in the VentureMind SafetyNet KYC documentation.

### 1.3 Suspicious Activity Reporting
Any transaction, pattern of behaviour, or activity that raises suspicion of money laundering, terrorist financing, or other financial crime shall be immediately escalated to the Compliance Officer and, where required by applicable law, reported to the relevant Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).

### 1.4 AML Record-Keeping
All AML-related records, including KYC documentation, transaction records, suspicious activity reports, and correspondent communications, shall be retained for a minimum of five (5) years (or longer as required by applicable law) in a secure, immutable format.

## SECTION 2 — DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

### 2.1 Applicable Frameworks
The Company shall comply with all applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to:
- General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR);
- UK GDPR;
- Personal Information Protection Law (China);
- Data Protection Act (various jurisdictions);
- Any additional privacy legislation applicable to the Company's operations or Users.

### 2.2 Lawful Basis for Processing
All processing of personal data must have a valid lawful basis as defined by applicable data protection law. The Company shall maintain a Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) documenting the lawful basis for each category of processing.

### 2.3 Data Subject Rights
The Company shall ensure that all data subjects can effectively exercise their rights, including the rights to:
1. Be informed about the collection and use of their personal data;
2. Access their personal data;
3. Rectify inaccurate personal data;
4. Erase their personal data ("right to be forgotten");
5. Restrict processing;
6. Data portability;
7. Object to processing;
8. Not be subject to automated decision-making with significant effects.

### 2.4 Breach Notification
Data breaches shall be notified to the relevant supervisory authority within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the breach. Where the breach poses a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, affected data subjects shall also be notified without undue delay.

### 2.5 Cross-Border Data Transfers
Transfers of personal data to third countries (outside the EEA/UK) shall only occur under approved transfer mechanisms:
- EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs);
- UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs);
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs);
- Adequacy decisions by the European Commission or UK government.

## SECTION 3 — TAX COMPLIANCE

### 3.1 General Tax Obligations
The Company and its entities shall comply with all applicable tax laws, including:
- Corporate income tax obligations;
- Value-added tax (VAT) and goods and services tax (GST);
- Withholding tax obligations;
- Employment taxes and social security contributions;
- Digital services taxes (where applicable).

### 3.2 Economic Substance
Each Company entity shall maintain adequate economic substance in its jurisdiction of incorporation, including:
1. adequate employees, directors, and physical presence;
2. genuine management and decision-making in the jurisdiction;
3. proportionate overheads relative to the entity's activities.

### 3.3 Country-by-Country Reporting
Where the Company qualifies as a multinational enterprise group with consolidated group revenue exceeding the applicable threshold (currently €750 million), the Company shall prepare and file Country-by-Country Reports (CbCR) in accordance with OECD BEPS Action 13.

### 3.4 Tax Advisory Services Disclaimer
Any tax advisory services provided through the Platform are based on the tax laws in effect at the time of the advisory. Tax laws are subject to change, sometimes with retroactive effect. The Company shall not be liable for any tax liability arising from a User's reliance on Platform-provided tax advice where such advice was correct at the time it was provided but was rendered incorrect by a subsequent change in law. Users are advised to consult qualified local tax counsel before making tax-related decisions.

## SECTION 4 — CORPORATE GOVERNANCE

### 4.1 Good Standing
Each Company entity shall maintain its good standing in its jurisdiction of incorporation, including:
- Timely filing of annual returns and accounts;
- Maintenance of registered offices and registered agents;
- Conduct of shareholder and board meetings as required by applicable corporate law;
- Maintenance of share registers and-minute books.

### 4.2 Director Obligations
Directors owe fiduciary duties to the Company, including duties of:
1. Care and diligence;
2. Good faith and proper purpose;
3. Avoidance of conflicts of interest;
4. Not fettering discretion;
5. Acting within authority.

### 4.3 Related-Party Transactions
All transactions between Company entities, or between the Company and its Directors, Shareholders, or associates, must be:
1. Conducted at fair market value;
2. Documented in writing;
3. Disclosed to the board or shareholders as required by applicable law;
4. Approved by disinterested parties where conflicts of interest exist.

---

## SECTION 5 — REGULATORY REPORTING

### 5.1 Ongoing Obligations
The Company shall monitor and comply with all ongoing regulatory reporting obligations in each jurisdiction where it operates or has entities, including:
- Financial reporting and audit requirements;
- Regulatory capital requirements (where applicable);
- Periodic regulatory filings;
- Notification of material changes to the Company's business or structure.

### 5.2 Regulatory Change Management
The Compliance Officer shall maintain a regulatory change log, monitoring proposed and enacted changes to applicable laws and regulations on a quarterly basis and assessing their impact on the Company's operations.

---

## SECTION 6 — COMPLIANCE GOVERNANCE

### 6.1 Compliance Officer
The Company shall designate a Compliance Officer responsible for:
1. Maintaining and updating this Framework;
2. Ensuring all staff are trained on compliance obligations;
3. Overseeing KYC/AML processes;
4. Managing regulatory relationships;
5. Reporting compliance matters to the Board.

### 6.2 Training
All employees with compliance-relevant roles shall complete mandatory compliance training within thirty (30) days of joining and annually thereafter. Training records shall be maintained for a minimum of three (3) years.

### 6.3 Annual Compliance Review
The Company shall conduct an annual compliance review, auditing adherence to this Framework, identifying gaps, and implementing corrective actions. The findings of the annual compliance review shall be reported to the Board.
