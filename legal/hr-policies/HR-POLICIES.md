## SECTION 1 — EQUALITY AND NON-DISCRIMINATION

### 1.1 Policy Statement
The Company is committed to providing equal opportunities in employment and to creating an inclusive, respectful, and discrimination-free workplace. No employee or job applicant shall be discriminated against on the basis of race, colour, ethnicity, nationality, national origin, religion, belief, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, marital or civil partnership status, age, disability, genetic information, membership of a trade union, or any other status protected by applicable law.

### 1.2 Prohibition
It is strictly prohibited to discriminate, harass, victimise, or otherwise disadvantage any employee on any of the grounds described in Section 1.1. This prohibition applies in all aspects of employment, including recruitment, compensation, promotion, training, discipline, and termination.

### 1.3 Reasonable Adjustments
The Company will make reasonable adjustments to ensure that employees with disabilities can perform their duties effectively. Employees requiring adjustments should notify their line manager or HR in writing.

### 1.4 Complaints Procedure
Any employee who believes they have been discriminated against should report the matter to HR in writing. All complaints will be investigated promptly, impartially, and with appropriate confidentiality. Retaliation against any employee who makes a good-faith complaint is strictly prohibited and will itself constitute a disciplinary matter.

## SECTION 2 — HEALTH AND SAFETY

### 2.1 Duty of Care
The Company owes a duty of care to provide and maintain a safe working environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors. This duty includes providing adequate training, equipment, and emergency procedures.

### 2.2 Employee Responsibilities
Each employee is responsible for:
1. Taking reasonable care of their own health and safety and that of others who may be affected by their actions;
2. Using provided personal protective equipment as directed;
3. Reporting any hazard, accident, or near-miss to their line manager or HR within twenty-four (24) hours;
4. Not wilfully interfering with or misusing any safety equipment or procedure.

### 2.3 Remote Work
Employees working remotely are responsible for ensuring their home office setup meets applicable ergonomic and safety standards. The Company will provide a one-time home-office equipment allowance of $__________.

## SECTION 4 — CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION

### 4.1 Duty of Confidentiality
All information relating to the Company's business, finances, technology, customers, suppliers, employees, and contractors that is not publicly available is strictly confidential. This duty continues during and after the termination of employment. Breach of confidentiality obligations may result in disciplinary action and/or legal proceedings.

### 4.2 Data Protection
The Company collects and processes employee personal data in accordance with its Privacy Policy and applicable data protection law, including the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) where applicable. Employees have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing, and port their personal data. Requests should be submitted to HR.

### 4.3 Acceptable Use of Company Systems
Company-provided systems, devices, email accounts, and network access are for business purposes only. Incidental personal use is permitted where it does not interfere with business operations, incur significant cost, or violate any other Company policy. The Company reserves the right to monitor use of its systems in accordance with applicable law.

## SECTION 5 — DISCIPLINE AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 Disciplinary Procedure
Minor issues will be addressed through informal coaching. Persistent or serious issues will be addressed through a formal disciplinary process:
1. **First written warning**: For conduct that does not meet expected standards.
2. **Final written warning**: For continued misconduct or serious misconduct.
3. **Termination**: For the most serious breaches or continued failure to improve after warnings.

### 5.2 Gross Misconduct
The following acts constitute gross misconduct and may result in immediate termination without warning:
- Theft, fraud, or embezzlement;
- Wilful damage to Company property;
- Serious breach of confidentiality obligations;
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying;
- Being under the influence of alcohol or drugs in the workplace;
- Serious insubordination;
- Material breach of the Employment Agreement.

### 5.3 Right to Appeal
Any employee who receives a written warning or termination notice has the right to appeal in writing within ten (10) working days. Appeals will be heard by a more senior manager or HR representative not involved in the original decision.

### 5.4 Performance Management
Persistent failure to meet performance standards (following a performance improvement plan) may result in termination. Performance issues will be documented, communicated, and given a reasonable opportunity to improve.

### 6.1 Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely with the prior written approval of their line manager. Remote work arrangements are subject to:
1. Maintenance of a dedicated, secure workspace with reliable internet connectivity;
2. Availability during core hours of [__] to [__] (local time of the Company);
3. Use of Company-approved VPN and security tools;
4. Attendance at in-person meetings or events as reasonably required.

### 6.2 Cross-Border Work
Employees working from a country other than their country of employment for more than thirty (30) consecutive days must notify HR in advance. Such arrangements may have tax, social security, and immigration implications that the employee is responsible for addressing, with the Company's support.

## SECTION 7 — ANTI-BRIBERY AND CORRUPTION

### 7.1 Zero Tolerance
The Company maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward bribery, corruption, and related practices. No employee may offer, promise, give, solicit, or receive any bribe, facilitation payment, kickback, or other improper advantage in connection with Company business.

### 7.2 Gifts and Hospitality
Gifts and hospitality are subject to the following limits:
- Individual gifts: not to exceed $__________ USD per annum per giver;
- Business meals: permitted when directly connected to business discussions;
- Entertainment and travel: requires prior written approval from management.

### 7.3 Reporting
Employees who become aware of any actual or suspected bribery or corruption must report it immediately through the Company's confidential reporting channel.
