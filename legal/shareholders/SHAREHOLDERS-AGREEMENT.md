## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Shareholders wish to define their rights, obligations, and relationships as shareholders of the Company, which operates within the VentureMind/Nomad Flow ecosystem;

## SECTION 1 — CAPITAL STRUCTURE

### 1.1 Authorised Share Capital
The Company shall have an authorised share capital as set out in its constitutional documents. The Shareholders may increase or decrease the authorised share capital only by a shareholders' resolution passed by at least seventy-five percent (75%) of all votes cast.

### 1.2 Share Register
The Company shall maintain a share register (the "Share Register") in accordance with the laws of the jurisdiction of incorporation. The Share Register shall record:
1. The full legal name and address of each Shareholder;
2. The number, class, and series of shares held by each Shareholder;
3. The date of issuance and any applicable vesting schedule;
4. Any encumbrances, liens, or charges over the shares;
5. Any transfer restrictions or lock-up periods applicable to each share.

The Company Secretary (if any) or, in the absence of a Company Secretary, a director designated by the Board, shall ensure the Share Register is updated within three (3) business days of any issuance, transfer, or modification.

### 1.3 Share Classes
The Company may issue shares in different classes with different rights. The rights attached to each class shall be as set out in the constitutional documents and summarised in **Schedule B**. No new class of shares shall be created without a shareholders' resolution passed by at least seventy-five percent (75%) of all votes cast.

## SECTION 2 — TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

### 2.1 Lock-Up Period
All shares issued to Shareholders (other than founders and early employees with pre-existing arrangements) shall be subject to a lock-up period of twelve (12) months from the date of issuance, during which such shares shall not be transferred, sold, pledged, or otherwise disposed of.

### 2.2 Right of First Refusal (ROFR)
Prior to any proposed transfer of shares, the transferring Shareholder must comply with the following procedure:

1. **Notice**: The transferring Shareholder must deliver written notice (the "Transfer Notice") to the Company Secretary and all other Shareholders, specifying:
   a. The number of shares proposed to be transferred;
   b. The identity of the proposed transferee;
   c. The proposed price per share;
   d. All material terms and conditions of the proposed transfer;
   e. The proposed closing date.

2. **ROFR Exercise Period**: Each non-transferring Shareholder shall have twenty-one (21) days from receipt of the Transfer Notice to elect to purchase all or any portion of the shares proposed to be transferred, on the same terms and conditions as set out in the Transfer Notice. If multiple non-transferring Shareholders elect to purchase, the shares shall be allocated among them pro rata in proportion to their then-current shareholdings.

3. **Company ROFR**: If no Shareholder elects to purchase within the exercise period, the Company shall have a further seven (7) days to elect to purchase, on the same terms.

4. **Failure to Exercise**: If no Shareholder and no eligible party exercises the ROFR within the applicable periods, the transferring Shareholder may proceed with the transfer to the proposed transferee, provided that:
   a. The transfer is completed within ninety (90) days of the expiry of the ROFR exercise period;
   b. The transfer is on terms no more favourable to the transferee than those set out in the Transfer Notice;
   c. The proposed transferee executes a deed of adherence agreeing to be bound by this Agreement;
   d. The transfer complies with all applicable laws and does not cause the Company to lose any material licence, permit, or regulatory status.

### 2.3 Permitted Transfers
Notwithstanding Section 2.2, shares may be transferred without triggering the ROFR:
1. To a trust established solely for the benefit of the transferring Shareholder and/or their immediate family members (spouse, children, parents), provided that the transferring Shareholder retains sole dispositive control over such trust;
2. By way of intestacy or testamentary succession to the estate of a deceased Shareholder;
3. With the prior written consent of all other Shareholders.

Any permitted transferee shall be required to execute a deed of adherence as a condition of such transfer.

### 2.4 Forced Transfer — Bad Actor
If a Shareholder is removed for Cause, convicted of a criminal offence involving fraud or dishonesty, or found to have materially breached this Agreement or any applicable law:
1. All unvested shares held by such Shareholder shall be immediately forfeited;
2. The Company shall have the right to repurchase all vested shares held by such Shareholder at a price equal to fifty percent (50%) of the Fair Market Value, as determined by an independent valuator;
3. The Company shall have the right to repurchase such shares using any available funds, including funds advanced by the remaining Shareholders as interest-free loans to the Company.

## SECTION 3 — BOARD GOVERNANCE

### 3.1 Board Composition
The Board shall comprise:
1. One (1) director seat per Shareholder holding more than ten percent (10%) of the issued and outstanding shares;
2. Up to three (3) independent directors appointed by a shareholders' resolution passed by a majority of votes cast;
3. A minimum of one (1) director seat designated for an individual with specific expertise in each of: (a) AI/technology, (b) financial services regulation, and (c) international tax.

### 3.2 Director Removal
Any director may be removed:
1. By the Shareholder who appointed them, at any time upon written notice;
2. By a shareholders' resolution passed by at least seventy-five percent (75%) of all votes cast.

A director removed under Section 3.2(2) may seek indemnification from the Company for any losses arising from such removal if the removal was without cause.

### 3.3 Board Decision-Making

| Decision Type | Threshold |
|--------------|-----------|
| Routine operational decisions | Simple majority |
| Related-party transactions | 75% + independent director approval |
| New share issuance | 75% (see dilution provisions) |
| Change to constitutional documents | 90% |
| Merger, acquisition, or sale of Company | 90% |
| Voluntary dissolution | Unanimous |
| Appointment or removal of Compliance Auditor | 75% |

### 3.4 Reserved Matters
In addition to Section 3.3, the following actions shall require prior approval by a shareholders' resolution passed by at least seventy-five percent (75%) of all votes cast:
1. Entry into any new jurisdiction requiring corporate or regulatory registration;
2. Any AML/KYC policy change;
3. Integration of any new AI agent or third-party API with access to customer PII;
4. Any data sharing arrangement with a third party involving personal data;
5. Any material change to the Platform's security architecture;
6. Any regulatory filing that could result in the Company becoming subject to licensing or authorisation requirements in any jurisdiction.

## SECTION 4 — ANTI-DILUTION AND PRE-EMPTION

### 4.1 Pre-Emption Rights on New Issuance
Prior to any issuance of new shares or convertible securities, the Company must offer such shares to existing Shareholders in proportion to their then-current shareholdings. Each Shareholder shall have twenty-one (21) days to elect to participate in the new issuance. Shares not taken up by existing Shareholders may be issued to third parties on terms no more favourable than those offered to existing Shareholders.

### 4.2 Broad-Based Weighted Average Anti-Dilution
In the event of any down-round financing (i.e., a priced round in which the price per share is less than the price per share in the most recent prior priced round), the anti-dilution provisions of the Founder Agreement shall apply to all Shareholders who are also founders, and to all investors who have executed anti-dilution protection agreements.

### 4.3 Dilution Cap for New Employees
No options or equity grants to employees, contractors, or advisors shall be made that would, in aggregate, dilute existing Shareholders' interests by more than fifteen percent (15%) of their then-current percentages, without the prior written consent of each Shareholder holding more than five percent (5%) of the issued and outstanding shares.

### 4.4 Wash-Out Round Protection
In the event of an up-round financing in which existing investors do not participate pro rata:
1. Any investor who does not participate in the up-round shall have their anti-dilution protection (if any) calculated as if the price per share in the up-round had been achieved in the immediately preceding round;
2. This wash-out provision is intended to prevent dilution of non-participating investors in scenarios where the round is structured to benefit participating investors.

## SECTION 5 — INFORMATION AND REPORTING

### 5.1 Monthly Reports
The Board shall cause the Company to deliver to each Shareholder, within fifteen (15) days of each month-end:
1. Management accounts comprising at minimum: revenue, operating expenses, net cash position, and burn rate;
2. An operational update summarising key platform metrics, user growth, and compliance activities;
3. A summary of any regulatory filings made or received during the month.

### 5.2 Quarterly Accounts
Within thirty (30) days of each quarter-end, the Company shall deliver to each Shareholder unaudited financial statements (comprising balance sheet, profit and loss account, and cash flow statement), together with a comparison to budget.

### 5.3 Annual Audit
Within ninety (90) days of each financial year-end, the Company shall deliver to each Shareholder audited financial statements prepared by an independent certified public accounting firm approved by the Board.

### 5.4 Inspection Rights
Each Shareholder holding more than five percent (5%) of the issued and outstanding shares shall have the right, upon not less than five (5) business days' written notice and during normal business hours, to inspect:
1. The Company's books and records;
2. The Share Register;
3. The minutes of Board and Shareholder meetings;
4. All material contracts to which the Company is a party.

This inspection right shall not be exercised more than twice in any twelve (12)-month period unless the inspecting Shareholder has reasonable grounds to suspect a breach of this Agreement or applicable law.

### 5.5 Annual General Meeting
An Annual General Meeting shall be held within six (6) months of each financial year-end. The agenda shall include:
1. Presentation of the audited financial statements;
2. Board report on the Company's performance;
3. Any ordinary and extraordinary resolutions of which notice has been given;
4. Election or re-election of directors.

## SECTION 6 — DIVIDEND AND DISTRIBUTION POLICY

### 6.1 Dividend Declaration
Dividends may be declared by a Board resolution and shall be paid to Shareholders pro rata in proportion to their shareholdings as of the record date. No dividend shall be declared unless the Company has sufficient distributable profits and the Compliance Auditor Agent has certified that such distribution does not impair the Company's regulatory capital requirements.

### 6.2 Withholding Tax
The Company shall be entitled to deduct and withhold from any dividend or distribution such amounts as are required to be deducted or withheld under the laws of any applicable jurisdiction, including but not limited to foreign withholding tax requirements. The Company shall remit such amounts to the relevant tax authority and shall provide the affected Shareholder with documentation of such withholding.

### 6.3 Return of Capital
Any return of capital to Shareholders shall require:
1. A Board resolution recommending the return of capital;
2. A shareholders' resolution passed by at least seventy-five percent (75%) of all votes cast;
3. Compliance with all applicable legal requirements in the jurisdiction of incorporation.

---

## SECTION 7 — WINDING UP AND DISSOLUTION

### 7.1 Voluntary Winding Up
The Company may be wound up voluntarily only by a shareholders' resolution passed by the unanimous consent of all Shareholders entitled to vote.

### 7.2 Distribution of Assets on Winding Up
On a winding up, the assets of the Company shall be applied in the following order of priority:
1. All amounts owed to creditors of the Company (other than Shareholders in their capacity as creditors);
2. Any accrued and unpaid dividends;
3. The return of paid-up capital to Shareholders pro rata;
4. Any surplus shall be distributed to Shareholders pro rata.

### 7.3 Compliance Auditor Role on Winding Up
Prior to any winding up, the Compliance Auditor Agent shall:
1. Produce a compliance wind-down plan addressing all outstanding regulatory obligations;
2. File all required final regulatory returns and deregistrations;
3. Obtain sign-off from legal counsel in each jurisdiction of operation;
4. Certify that all customer data has been handled in accordance with the applicable data protection laws and the Privacy Policy.
