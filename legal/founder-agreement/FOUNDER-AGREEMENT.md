### 2.2 Anti-Dilution Protection — Broad-Based Weighted Average
In the event of any future equity financing, the Founders' respective percentages shall be subject to dilution pursuant to a broad-based weighted average anti-dilution formula, unless the affected Founder consents in writing to a different arrangement. No dilutive event shall occur without the prior written consent of the affected Founder if such event would reduce their equity interest by more than twenty percent (20%) of their then-current percentage.

### 2.3 Equity Pool Reserve
A minimum of ten percent (10%) of fully-diluted equity shall be reserved for the Employee Stock Option Pool ("ESOP"). The ESOP pool shall not be diluted by the Founders' personal contributions; dilution shall be borne proportionally by all shareholders. Any grant of options exceeding the ESOP pool requires unanimous Founder consent.

### 2.4 Vesting Schedule — Four-Year with One-Year Cliff
Each Founder's unvested shares shall be subject to the following vesting schedule:
- **Vesting commencement date**: The Effective Date of this Agreement.
- **Cliff period**: Twelve (12) months from the vesting commencement date. No shares vest during the cliff period.
- **Post-cliff vesting**: Upon completion of twelve (12) months of service, twenty-five percent (25%) of such Founder's shares shall vest.
- **Remaining vesting**: The remaining seventy-five percent (75%) shall vest in equal monthly installments over the subsequent thirty-six (36) months.
- **Continuous service required**: Vesting requires continuous service to the Company. Any period of leave exceeding ninety (90) consecutive days (other than statutorily mandated leave) shall pause the vesting clock for the duration of such excess leave.

### 2.5 Acceleration Events
#### 2.5.1 Single-Trigger Acceleration
Upon a Change of Control (as defined in Section 9.3), one hundred percent (100%) of all unvested shares of all Founders shall immediately vest.

#### 2.5.2 Double-Trigger Acceleration
In the absence of a Change of Control, if a Founder is terminated Without Cause (as defined in Section 6.2) or resigns for Good Reason (as defined in Section 6.3), fifty percent (50%) of such Founder's unvested shares shall vest, subject to the following additional conditions:
- The terminated or resigning Founder must execute a full and unconditional general release of claims in favour of the Company and the other Founder within thirty (30) days of termination;
- The terminated or resigning Founder must return all Company property, confidential information, and proprietary materials within seven (7) days of termination;
- The terminated or resigning Founder must not directly or indirectly compete with the Company for a period of twelve (12) months following termination (subject to applicable law);
- The terminated or resigning Founder must not solicit Company employees, contractors, clients, or partners for twelve (12) months following termination.

#### 2.5.3 No Acceleration on Voluntary Resignation Without Good Reason
Voluntary resignation without Good Reason shall result in immediate forfeiture of all unvested shares. There shall be no exception to this provision unless agreed in writing by all Parties.

### 3.2 Valuation of Non-Cash Contributions
Non-cash contributions (including but not limited to code, IP, business relationships, introductions, and services rendered prior to this Agreement) shall be valued at fair market value as determined by an independent third-party valuation expert appointed by mutual agreement of the Founders. The cost of such valuation shall be borne by the contributing Founder unless the contribution is valued at greater than $25,000 USD, in which case the cost shall be borne by the Company.

### 3.3 Failure to Fund
If any Founder fails to contribute the agreed cash capital within thirty (30) days of the due date (the "Contribution Due Date"), the following graduated consequences apply:
- **Days 1–30**: The non-contributing Founder's voting rights shall be proportionally reduced to reflect the shortfall.
- **Days 31–60**: The non-contributing Founder's equity vesting shall pause until the contribution is received. During this pause, the contributing Founder may elect to cover the shortfall by advancing a loan to the Company at a rate of twelve percent (12%) per annum.
- **Days 61+**: The non-contributing Founder's unvested shares shall be subject to dilution at a forty percent (40%) discount to the most recent third-party valuation. The contributing Founder may, at their sole election, either (a) acquire the diluted shares at such discounted price, or (b) require the non-contributing Founder to forfeit their right to such shares, which shall be returned to the Company treasury for reissuance.
- The contributing Founder may waive any of the foregoing in writing, which waiver shall not constitute a precedent or admission.

### 3.4 Loans Between Founders
Any advance of funds between Founders (other than the cash contributions described above) shall be documented by a separate written loan agreement specifying the principal amount, interest rate (which shall not be below the applicable IRS prescribed rate), repayment terms, and security (if any). Verbal agreements regarding loans are void and unenforceable.

## SECTION 5 — DECISION-MAKING AND GOVERNANCE

### 5.1 Board of Directors
The Company shall have a Board of Directors (the "Board") comprising:
- One (1) seat per Founder;
- Up to two (2) independent directors appointed by mutual agreement of the Founders;
- One (1) observer seat for any investor holding more than ten percent (10%) of the Company's fully-diluted equity (non-voting, non-counted toward quorum).

### 5.2 Quorum and Voting
- Ordinary resolutions require a simple majority of directors present in person or by proxy at a duly convened meeting.
- Extraordinary resolutions (as listed in Section 5.3) require the affirmative vote of at least seventy-five percent (75%) of all directors.
- Each director shall have one (1) vote. There shall be no weighted voting by directors.
- Quorum requires the presence of at least sixty percent (60%) of all directors. If quorum is not achieved within thirty (30) minutes of the scheduled meeting time, the meeting shall be adjourned for seven (7) days, at which time quorum shall be deemed achieved regardless of attendance.

### 5.3 Extraordinary Resolutions Requiring 75% Approval
The following actions shall require the affirmative vote of at least 75% of all directors:
1. Issuance of new shares or convertible securities;
2. Amendment of this Agreement or the Company's constitutional documents;
3. Acquisition or disposal of assets or businesses valued at more than $50,000 USD;
4. Incurring indebtedness exceeding $25,000 USD (excluding ordinary-course trade payables);
5. Entry into any exclusive licensing arrangement;
6. Merger, consolidation, or sale of the Company;
7. Voluntary winding up or dissolution;
8. Appointment or removal of the Compliance Auditor Agent or the Watchdog Agent (as defined in the Platform's SafetyNet Protocol);
9. Material change to the Platform's compliance architecture;
10. Any transaction with a related party.

### 5.4 Deadlock Resolution
In the event of a deadlock on any Board resolution (including routine resolutions) that persists for more than thirty (30) days, the following escalation procedure shall apply:
- **Step 1 — Founder Negotiation**: The Founders shall meet in good faith to resolve the deadlock within fourteen (14) days.
- **Step 2 — Mediated Resolution**: If Step 1 fails, the Founders shall engage a mutually agreed-upon mediator to facilitate resolution within a further fourteen (14) days.
- **Step 3 — Expert Determination**: If Step 2 fails, the matter shall be referred to an independent expert (e.g., a qualified business valuator or industry specialist) whose determination shall be binding and final, provided that neither Party shall be required to accept terms materially worse than their last offer.
- **Step 4 — Texas Shootout**: If after ninety (90) days from the commencement of deadlock the matter remains unresolved, any Founder may initiate a "Texas Shootout" mechanism: one Founder must make a written offer to buy out the other Founder's entire equity stake at a specific price. The other Founder then has fourteen (14) days to either accept the offer and sell their shares, or elect to buy the offering Founder's shares at the same price. The initiating Founder's offer must be made in good faith and must represent a genuine offer to purchase, not a tool of coercion.

### 5.5 Information Rights
Each Founder, as a director, shall be entitled to receive:
- Monthly management accounts within fifteen (15) days of month-end;
- Quarterly financial statements within thirty (30) days of quarter-end;
- Annual audited financial statements within ninety (90) days of financial year-end;
- Advance notice of all Board meetings (minimum fourteen (14) days for regular meetings, minimum forty-eight (48) hours for emergency meetings);
- Access to all Company books, records, and data upon reasonable notice during business hours.

## SECTION 6 — TERMINATION AND EXIT

### 6.1 Termination for Cause
A Founder's engagement with the Company may be terminated for Cause by the Board (excluding the affected Founder) with immediate effect upon written notice. "Cause" shall mean:
1. Conviction of, or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to, any criminal offence involving moral turpitude, fraud, or dishonesty;
2. Willful misconduct resulting in material financial harm to the Company;
3. Breach of fiduciary duty amounting to personal profit at the Company's expense;
4. Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information or trade secrets;
5. Material breach of this Agreement that is not cured within fourteen (14) days of written notice;
6. Failure to perform duties competently after written notice and a thirty (30)-day cure period;
7. Conduct constituting gross negligence or willful misconduct in the performance of duties;
8. Discriminatory, harassing, or retaliatory conduct in violation of applicable law;
9. Violation of any anti-corruption or anti-money laundering law applicable to the Company.

### 6.2 Termination Without Cause
The Company may terminate any Founder's engagement Without Cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice, subject to the payment of severance as follows:
- Three (3) months' base salary in the currency of the Company's principal operations;
- Reimbursement of all business expenses properly incurred and documented prior to the date of termination;
- Acceleration of twenty-five percent (25%) of such Founder's unvested shares (in addition to any vested shares).

### 6.3 Resignation for Good Reason
A Founder may resign for Good Reason, which shall mean:
1. A material reduction in role or responsibilities without the affected Founder's written consent;
2. A reduction in base compensation exceeding twenty percent (20%) without the affected Founder's written consent;
3. A change in the principal place of work by more than fifty (50) kilometres without the affected Founder's written consent;
4. A material breach of this Agreement by the Company or the other Founder that is not cured within fourteen (14) days of written notice;
5. The other Founder's conviction for an offence referred to in Section 6.1(1).

Resignation for Good Reason shall entitle the resigning Founder to the same severance as Termination Without Cause.

### 6.4 Voluntary Resignation Without Good Reason
A Founder who resigns without Good Reason:
- Shall forfeit all unvested shares as of the date of resignation;
- Shall remain bound by the non-compete provisions of Section 8.1 and the non-solicitation provisions of Section 8.2;
- Shall not be entitled to severance or any payment beyond accrued salary and reimbursable expenses;
- Must provide a minimum of sixty (60) days' written notice;
- May be placed on garden leave during the notice period at the Company's discretion.

### 6.5 Garden Leave
During any notice period (whether given by the Company or the Founder), the Company may place the departing Founder on garden leave, during which:
- The departing Founder shall be relieved of all duties and shall not represent themselves as a director, employee, or representative of the Company;
- The departing Founder shall remain on the Company's payroll but shall not be entitled to any new equity grants;
- The Company may require the departing Founder to surrender all Company property and access credentials during the garden leave period.

## SECTION 7 — NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

### 7.1 Non-Compete
#### 7.1.1 Scope
For a period of twelve (12) months following the termination of any Founder's engagement with the Company (regardless of the cause of such termination), the departing Founder shall not, directly or indirectly:
1. Carry on, be engaged in, have any financial interest in, or provide services to any business that competes with the Company or the Platform in any jurisdiction where the Company operates or has active users;
2. Own more than a five percent (5%) passive equity interest in a competing entity listed on a regulated stock exchange;
3. Solicit, canvass, or solicit business from any person or entity that was a client, customer, or active lead of the Company within the twelve (12) months preceding the Founder's termination;
4. Interfere with, disrupt, or attempt to disrupt any contract, relationship, or agreement between the Company and any third party.

#### 7.1.2 Limitations and Savings
The non-compete shall only apply to businesses that directly compete with the Platform as described in the Platform's published description. It shall not prevent the departing Founder from:
- Working in a different industry or vertical;
- Providing services to a non-competing business within the same industry;
- Continuing to work with pre-existing clients of the departing Founder's prior business (provided such clients are not also clients of the Company and provided that the departing Founder did not bring such clients to the Company).

#### 7.1.3 Enforcement and Savings
If any court of competent jurisdiction determines that any portion of this non-compete is overly broad, void, or unenforceable under applicable law, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect, and the scope of the non-compete shall be deemed automatically modified to the maximum scope permitted by applicable law in the relevant jurisdiction.

### 7.2 Non-Solicitation
For a period of twelve (12) months following termination, no Founder shall:
1. Solicit, divert, or hire away any employee, contractor, or consultant of the Company, or any person who was an employee, contractor, or consultant within the six (6) months preceding termination;
2. Directly or indirectly solicit or attempt to influence any investor, strategic partner, or service provider of the Company to reduce, terminate, or not renew their relationship with the Company;
3. Make any disparaging statement (whether written or oral) about the Company, the Platform, or the other Founder.

### 7.3 Clawback
If any Founder breaches the non-compete or non-solicitation provisions, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity:
- The breaching Founder shall forfeit all unvested shares as of the date of breach;
- The breaching Founder shall pay the Company a sum equal to the greater of (a) the turnover generated by the breaching activity during the twelve (12) months preceding the breach, and (b) $50,000 USD, as a contractual estimate of damages;
- The Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief without the requirement to post a bond, and without prejudice to any other remedy.

## SECTION 8 — CONFIDENTIALITY

### 8.1 Definition of Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" means all non-public information, in any form, disclosed by the Company or one Founder to the other, whether before or after the Effective Date, including but not limited to:
- The Platform's technical architecture, source code, algorithms, and trade secrets;
- Customer lists, lead data, and marketing strategies;
- Business plans, financial projections, and pricing models;
- The contents of this Agreement and any Schedule hereto;
- Any information marked "Confidential" or "Proprietary."

### 8.2 Exceptions
Confidential Information shall not include information that:
1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Founder;
2. Was lawfully in the receiving Founder's possession prior to disclosure;
3. Is independently developed by the receiving Founder without reference to Confidential Information;
4. Is received from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation.

The burden of proving any exception rests with the receiving Founder.

### 8.3 Obligations
The receiving Founder agrees to:
1. Maintain Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care as they use to protect their own most sensitive confidential information (and in no event less than reasonable care);
2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Founder;
3. Use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of this Agreement;
4. Upon termination, return or destroy all Confidential Information (including copies) within seven (7) days;
5. Promptly notify the disclosing Founder of any known or suspected unauthorized disclosure or breach.

### 8.4 Duration
Confidentiality obligations shall survive the termination of this Agreement for an indefinite period, or until the relevant Confidential Information falls within one of the exceptions in Section 8.2.

## SECTION 9 — CHANGE OF CONTROL AND EXIT

### 9.1 Right of First Refusal (ROFR)
Prior to any sale of shares by a Founder, the selling Founder must offer the shares to the remaining Founder(s) and then to the Company (in that order) on the same terms as the proposed sale. Each party shall have fourteen (14) days to exercise the ROFR. If no party exercises the ROFR within such period, the selling Founder may proceed with the sale to the third party, subject to compliance with Sections 9.2 and 9.3.

### 9.2 Tag-Along Rights
In any sale of shares by a Founder to a third party, each remaining Founder shall have the right to "tag along" — i.e., to sell their proportional share of shares to the same third party on the same terms and conditions. The selling Founder must provide written notice to the remaining Founders of the proposed sale at least twenty-one (21) days prior to the anticipated closing date, specifying the price, terms, identity of the proposed purchaser, and all material conditions.

### 9.3 Change of Control Definition
"Change of Control" means:
1. A sale, transfer, or disposition of all or substantially all of the Company's assets;
2. A merger, consolidation, reverse merger, or other business combination in which the Company's pre-transaction shareholders hold less than fifty percent (50%) of the voting power of the surviving entity;
3. A transaction or series of related transactions in which any person or group acquires more than fifty percent (50%) of the Company's outstanding voting securities;
4. A transaction in which the Company ceases to be a reporting company or is delisted from any exchange.

### 9.4 Drag-Along Rights
If holders of at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the Company's issued and outstanding shares agree to a sale of the Company (a "Drag-Along Transaction"), all remaining shareholders shall be required to sell their shares on the same terms and conditions, provided that:
- The drag-along price per share is the same for all shareholders;
- There are no material representations or warranties given by individual shareholders that are more extensive than their pro rata share of the Company's representations and warranties;
- Each shareholder receives legal advice or a written waiver of the right to legal advice.
